<a id="type-ii-restriction-enzymes">Type II Restriction Enzymes</a> - These [restriction enzymes](#restriction-enzyme) are used to [cleave](#cleavage) double-stranded [DNA](#dna-desoxyribonucleic-acid) in a location that is outside the recognition sequence of the [enzyme](#enzyme). They are particularly useful for [DNA assembly](#dna-assembly), as they enable creating arbitrary [overhang](#overhang) sequences. Commonly used Type IIs [enzymes](#enzyme) include BsaI and BbsI, which are both used in [Golden Gate](#golden-gate) or [MoClo assembly](#moclo).
