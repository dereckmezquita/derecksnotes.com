<a id="anneal">Anneal</a> - In the context of [DNA assembly](#dna-assembly) this means the coming together of [complementary](#complementary) (or [near-complementary](#complementary), in some cases) sequences of [DNA](#dna-desoxyribonucleic-acid). This is often assisted by a change in temperature: two [complementary sequences](#complementary) are often heated up (to say 95°C) and then allowed to cool down. The heating removes self-annealing, which causes [secondary structures](#secondary-structure) to form in the [DNA](#dna-desoxyribonucleic-acid), which might [inhibit](#inhibitor) annealing to the other [molecule](#molecule). The cooling down allows the formation of [hydrogen bonds](#hydrogen-bond) between the two [DNA](#dna-desoxyribonucleic-acid) strands. The opposite process to annealing is melting or [denaturing](#denature) of [DNA](#dna-desoxyribonucleic-acid), which happens at high temperatures.
