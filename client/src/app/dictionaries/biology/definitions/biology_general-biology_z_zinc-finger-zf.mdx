<a id="zinc-finger-zf">Zinc Finger (ZF)</a> - A [DNA](#dna-desoxyribonucleic-acid) binding protein motif, often seen in [transcription factors](#transcription-factor-tf). In the folded motif, a zinc ion is coordinated by [amino acid](#amino-acid) helices and sheets. Many motifs bind sequence-specifically (3-4 [base pairs](#base-pair-bp)) and can be combined together to bind longer sequences (9-12 basepairs). While a "code" exist (mapping of motifs to sequences), technologies like [TALEs](#tale-s) or [CRISPR/Cas9](#crispr-cas9) are often preferred for engineering [DNA](#dna-desoxyribonucleic-acid) binding [proteins](#protein), as the code is more clear and specific in these; increasing the rate of success.
