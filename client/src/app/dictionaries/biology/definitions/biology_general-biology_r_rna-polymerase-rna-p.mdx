<a id="rna-polymerase-rna-p">RNA Polymerase (RNA-P)</a> - RNA polymerase; the [protein](#protein) complex that [transcribes](#transcription) [RNA](#rna) from [DNA](#dna-desoxyribonucleic-acid). In prokaryotes RNA-P, [promoter](#promoter) [specificity](#specificity) is determined by the presence of a sigma factor and the complex usually binds to the -35 and -10 elements (35[bp](#base-pair-bp) and 10[bp](#base-pair-bp) upstream of the start [codon](#codon)) of [genes](#gene). Occlusion of these sites through [transcription factor](#transcription-factor-tf) binding is a common mechanism of genetic [repression](#repressor). In [eukaryotes](#eukaryota), [mRNA](#mrna) [transcription](#transcription) is carried out by RNA polymerase II, but other RNA-P types are responsible for the production of [transfer RNAs (tRNA)](#t-rna), [ribosomal RNAs (rRNA)](#rrna), and some regulatory [RNAs](#regulatory-rna).
