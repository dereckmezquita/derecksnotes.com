<a id="dna-desoxyribonucleic-acid">DNA (Desoxyribonucleic Acid)</a> - A [molecule](#molecule) ([nucleic acid](#nucleic-acid)) which carries most of the genetic instructions used in the development, function and reproduction of all known living organisms and many [viruses](#virus). DNA is the primary compenent of [chromosomes](#chromosome).

<Figure src="/dictionaries/biology/dna.jpg">
    DNA a double helix molecule, the current known basis for life.
</Figure>

DNA is composed of two directional strands. The directionality of these strands is dictated by the orientation of the [nucleotides](#nucleotide) that compose it; there is a [5' end](#5-prime-end-5) and a [3' end](#5-prime-end-5).

Four different [nucleotides](#nucleotide) compose DNA, these are differentiated by the nucleic bases they hold: [adenine (A)](#adenine), [thymine (T)](#thymine), [guanine (G)](#guanine), and [cytosine (C)](#cytosine). These can grouped together chemically into the purine group A, T, and the pyrimidine group G, C. The two strands come together and bind through weak [hydrogen bonds](#hydrogen-bond); A - T (2 bonds), and C - G (3 bonds).
