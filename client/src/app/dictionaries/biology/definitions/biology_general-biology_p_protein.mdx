<a id="protein">Protein</a> - A [polymer](#polymer) of [amino acids](#amino-acid) that typically folds into a particular shape; though [proteins](#protein) with less-defined shapes exist. Some [proteins](#protein) are multimeric, meaning they are a complex of many copies of the same [amino acid](#amino-acid) sequences.

<Figure src="/dictionaries/biology/protein.png">
    Protein strucutre, primary structure is the sequence of amino acids, secondary α helices and β sheets, tertiary structure the association of secondary strucutres and quaternary strucutres the association of tertiary structures.
</Figure>

Many [proteins](#protein) are [enzymes](#enzyme), allowing them to [catalyse](#catalysation) specific reactions, others are structural. [Proteins](#protein) are created in the process of [translation](#translation) using information encoded by [codons](#codon) in a [messenger RNA](#mrna). Note that "natural" amino acids are L-amino acids (levogyre).
