<a id="metathesis_reaction">Metathesis Reaction</a> - Also known as a **double displacement** reaction where **two compounds** exchange **ions** or components to form **two new compounds**:

$$
\text{AX} + \text{BY} \;\to\; \text{AY} + \text{BX}
$$

- Common in [precipitation](#precipitation) (e.g., insoluble salts) or [acid_base](#acid_base) neutralisations.
- Must be a [balanced_equation](#balanced_equation) for stoichiometric correctness.
- Reaction extent can hinge on [solubility](#solubility) rules, forming a precipitate or non-volatile product that drives equilibrium forward.

<Alert type='note'>
Metathesis underscores how certain insoluble or weakly dissociating products shift equilibria, enabling rapid ion exchange in solution.
</Alert>
