<a id="logarithm">Logarithm</a> - The **inverse** of the **exponential** function, commonly used in **pH** or reaction rate expressions. For instance, pH $ = -\log [\text{H}^+] $:

- If $ y = \log_{10}(x) $, then $ 10^y = x $.
- Reaction rates in [collision_theory](#collision_theory) can appear in forms like $ \mathrm{ln}(k) = -E_a/(RT) + \text{constant} $ (Arrhenius expression).
- Different [units](#units) or bases: common log (base 10) vs. natural log (base e).  
- In pH ($ [\text{H}^+] $ in molÂ·L<sup>-1</sup>), a small change in $\log$ scale means large changes in $[\text{H}^+]$.

<Alert type='note'>
Logarithmic scales help handle wide dynamic ranges in concentration, reaction rates, or intensities.
