<a id="autoionization">Autoionization</a> - A process where **identical molecules** spontaneously ionise each other. The classic example is water:

$$
2\,\text{H}_2\text{O} \;\rightleftharpoons\; \text{H}_3\text{O}^+ + \text{OH}^-
$$

- **pH Scale**: Pure water's autoionization sets [arrhenius-acid](#arrhenius_acid) and [arrhenius-base](#arrhenius_base) concepts, yielding the ion-product $ K_w $.
- **Amphoteric**: Substances like water or certain [amino-acid](#amino_acid) side chains can display autoionization or partial self-dissociation.
- **Thermodynamic Variation**: Elevated temperatures can shift the equilibrium, affecting $ K_w $.

Autoionization underscores water's [amphoteric](#amphoteric) nature and is pivotal in discussions of buffer chemistry, acid-base equilibria, and advanced pH calculations.
