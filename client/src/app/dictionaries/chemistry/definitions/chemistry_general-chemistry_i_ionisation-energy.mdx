<a id="ionisation-energy">Ionisation Energy</a> - The **energy required** to **remove** an **electron** from a **neutral atom** in the **gas phase**:

$$
\mathrm{Atom} \rightarrow \mathrm{Atom}^+ + e^-
$$

- Typically measured in kJ/mol.
- Trends: Increases up a group (less shielding) and across a period (higher nuclear charge). Ties to [electronegativity](#electronegativity) and [electron_affinity](#electron-affinity).
- [bohr_model](#bohr-model) historically explained hydrogen's ionisation energy. Modern advanced [collision_theory](#collision-theory) considers multi-electron interactions.
