<a id="isotope">Isotope</a> - **Atoms** of the **same element** (same atomic number) but **different numbers** of **neutrons**, leading to distinct **mass numbers**. For example, $^{12}\mathrm{C}$, $^{13}\mathrm{C}$, $^{14}\mathrm{C}$.

- Can be stable or radioactive. Radioactive isotopes often relate to [half_life](#half_life) or [beta_decay](#beta_decay) processes.
- Mass differences can influence [binding_energy](#binding_energy) within the nucleus.
- Conversions between single-atom measures and macroscopic scales use the [avogadro_constant](#avogadro_constant).
- Chemical properties are generally similar (same electron configuration), but nuclear properties vary.
