<a id="indicator">Indicator</a> - A **substance** (often a **weak acid** or base) that **changes colour** in response to **pH** shifts, revealing if a solution is acidic, basic, or near neutral.

- **Examples**: Litmus, phenolphthalein, methyl orange.
- **Mechanism**: Indicator's molecular form and its conjugate differ in colour. Shifts in [ph_potential_hydrogen](#ph_potential_hydrogen) cause one form to dominate.
- **Relation**: In [arrhenius_acid](#arrhenius_acid)/[base](#base) contexts, the indicator monitors **[autoionization](#autoionization)** of water and H<sup>+</sup> changes in solution.

<Alert type='note'>
Indicators are pivotal in titrations, visually signalling the endpoint where acid-base neutralisation occurs.
</Alert>
