<a id="hard_water">Hard Water</a> - Water containing high **concentrations** of **calcium** and **magnesium** ions (often Ca<sup>2+</sup>, Mg<sup>2+</sup>), leading to **scale** build-up and soap scum formation.

- **Causes**: Limestone (CaCO<sub>3</sub>) dissolution, dolomite (MgCO<sub>3</sub>) presence.
- **Effects**: Reduced solubility of soaps, scale in boilers, interference in detergents.
- **Chemistry**: Complexation can reduce hardness. Chelating agents like [edta-complex](#edta_complex) or forming [complex_ion](#complex_ion) with Ca<sup>2+</sup> help soften water.
- **Relevance**: [solubility](#solubility) equilibria and partial [ionisation_energy](#ionisation_energy) considerations in mineral-laden environments.
