<a id="coulombs_law">Coulomb's Law</a> - Describes the **electrostatic force** ($F$) between two **point charges** $q_1$ and $q_2$, separated by distance $r$:

$$
F = k_e \frac{q_1 \, q_2}{r^2}
$$

- $k_e \approx 8.9875 \times 10^9 \,\text{N m}^2 \text{C}^{-2}$.  
- In ionic solids or solutions, explains [ionic_bond](#ionic_bond) strength.  
- Helps interpret electron-nucleus attraction in the [bohr_model](#bohr_model), and bridging single-charge to mole-based [avogadro_constant](#avogadro_constant) scales.  
- In electromagnetism, current measured in [ampere](#ampere) depends on charge flow and Coulombic definitions.
