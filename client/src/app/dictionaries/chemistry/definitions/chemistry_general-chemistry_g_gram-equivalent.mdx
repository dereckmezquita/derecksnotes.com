<a id="gram_equivalent">Gram-Equivalent</a> - A **measure** defined as the **mass** of a substance that reacts with or replaces **1 mole** of hydrogen ions (in acid-base) or **1 mole** of electrons (in [redox](#redox)).

- Historically used for normality (N) calculations: Normality = (grams of solute / litre) / (gram-equivalent mass).
- In [acid](#acid)-base contexts, a gram-equivalent neutralises 1 mole of H<sup>+</sup> or OH<sup>-</sup>.
- Ties to the [avogadro_constant](#avogadro_constant) for bridging ionic or electron-based reactions.
- Modern practice often prefers mole-based stoichiometry, but the concept still appears in older texts.

<Alert type='note'>
One gram-equivalent of a [base](#base) can supply 1 mole of OH<sup>-</sup>, while for [acid](#acid) it typically provides 1 mole of H<sup>+</sup>.
</Alert>
