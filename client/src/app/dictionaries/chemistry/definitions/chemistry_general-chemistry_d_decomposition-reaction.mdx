<a id="decomposition-reaction">Decomposition Reaction</a> - A **chemical reaction** in which **one compound** splits into **two** or more **simpler substances**. Often requires energy input (heat, light, or electricity).

Example:
$$
\text{CaCO}_3 \;\rightarrow\; \text{CaO} + \text{CO}_2
$$

- **Energy**: Usually needs a certain [activation_energy](#activation-energy) to break existing bonds.
- **Balancing**: Must be a [balanced_equation](#balanced-equation) for mass/atom conservation.
- **Kinetics**: [collision_theory](#collision-theory) clarifies that effective collisions or sufficient energy triggers bond-breaking.
- **Thermodynamics**: Reaction [enthalpy](#enthalpy) can be positive (endothermic) or negative (less common).
