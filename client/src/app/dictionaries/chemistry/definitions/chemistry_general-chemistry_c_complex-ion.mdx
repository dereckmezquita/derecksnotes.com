<a id="complex_ion">Complex Ion</a> - An **ion** formed by a **central metal** (often a transition metal) **bonded** to one or more **ligands** via coordinate (dative) bonds. Written typically as [M(L)<sub>n</sub>]<sup>charge</sup>.

- Example: $[\text{Fe}(\text{CN})_6]^{3-}$, $[\text{Cu}(\text{NH}_3)_4]^{2+}$.  
- Part of [coordination_chemistry](#coordination_chemistry), with the metal-ligand interactions sometimes approximated as a [covalent_bond](#covalent_bond) from the ligand side.  
- Ligands can behave like [arrhenius_base](#arrhenius_base) donors, supplying electron pairs to the metal.  
- In-depth discussion involves [dative_bond](#dative_bond) formation.

<Alert type='note'>
Complex ions underlie many biochemical metalloproteins (e.g., haemoglobin) and industrial catalysts.
</Alert>
