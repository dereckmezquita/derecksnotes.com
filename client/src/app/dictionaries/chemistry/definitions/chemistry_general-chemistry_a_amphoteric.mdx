<a id="amphoteric">Amphoteric</a> - A substance capable of reacting both as an acid and as a base. Water (H<sub>2</sub>O) is the quintessential example ([autoprotolysis](#autoprotolysis)), sometimes donating a proton, sometimes accepting one. Beyond water:

- **Metal oxides** such as ZnO and Al<sub>2</sub>O<sub>3</sub> can display amphoteric behaviour.  
- **[Acetate](#acetate)** can (under certain conditions) accept protons or donate them, albeit weakly.

In an [Arrhenius-acid](#arrhenius_acid) context, an amphoteric species may furnish H<sup>+</sup> ions, but in an [Arrhenius-base](#arrhenius_base) context, the same species may yield OH<sup>-</sup> or accept H<sup>+</sup>. This **dual nature** is central to many acid-base equilibrium processes.
