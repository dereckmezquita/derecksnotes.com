<a id="arrhenius_acid">Arrhenius Acid</a> - Defined as any substance that **increases the concentration of H<sup>+</sup> ions** in aqueous solution. It dissociates to yield protons:

$$
\text{HA} \;\to\; \text{H}^+ + \text{A}^-
$$

This theory, proposed by Svante Arrhenius, laid the groundwork for modern acid-base chemistry.  
**Examples**: HCl, H<sub>2</sub>SO<sub>4</sub>, and HNO<sub>3</sub>.  

Arrhenius also contributed the **Arrhenius equation** in kinetics, linking acid-base chemistry to [activation-energy](#activation_energy). An acid's behaviour complements that of an [Arrhenius-base](#arrhenius_base).
