<a id="arrhenius_base">Arrhenius Base</a> - A substance that **increases the concentration of OH<sup>-</sup> ions** when dissolved in water. Classic examples include NaOH, KOH, and Ca(OH)<sub>2</sub>. According to Arrhenius:

$$
\text{BOH} \;\to\; \text{B}^+ + \text{OH}^-
$$

An [Arrhenius Acid](#arrhenius_acid) introduces H<sup>+</sup>, while an Arrhenius base introduces OH<sup>-</sup>. When these ions combine, they yield H<sub>2</sub>O:

$$
\text{H}^+ + \text{OH}^- \;\to\; \text{H}_2\text{O}
$$
