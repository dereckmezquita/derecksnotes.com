<a id="job-method">Job's Method</a> - Also called the **method of continuous variations**, it determines **stoichiometric ratios** in **complex formation** or **binding** by varying **mole fractions** of reactants while maintaining total [concentration](#concentration) constant.

- Plotting a signal (e.g. absorbance in [spectroscopy](#spectroscopy), temperature change in [calorimetry](#calorimetry)) vs. mole fraction reveals the maximum, indicating the stoichiometry of the complex.
- Applied in [coordination_chemistry](#coordination-chemistry) to confirm ligand:metal ratios or in host-guest systems.
