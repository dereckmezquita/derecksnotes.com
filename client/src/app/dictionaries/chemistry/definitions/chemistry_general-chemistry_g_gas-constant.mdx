<a id="gas_constant">Gas Constant</a> - Symbolised by $R$, a **universal constant** in the **ideal gas equation**: $pV = nRT$. Numerically about $8.314\,\mathrm{J\,mol^{-1}\,K^{-1}}$.

- Relates pressure $p$, volume $V$, and temperature $T$ of a gas.
- Combines the [avogadro_constant](#avogadro_constant) and the Boltzmann constant $k_B$.
- Crucial for [daltons_law](#daltons_law) of partial pressures and expansions under [charles_law](#charles_law).
- Appears widely in thermodynamics (calculations for entropy, enthalpy, etc.).

<Alert type='important'>
Remember that different units (L·atm·K<sup>-1</sup>·mol<sup>-1</sup>, J·K<sup>-1</sup>·mol<sup>-1</sup>) can change numeric value but represent the same constant.
</Alert>
