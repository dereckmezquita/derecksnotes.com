<a id="gas-law">Gas Law</a> - A **collective term** for relationships describing the behaviour of **ideal gases**. It includes **Boyle's law** ($pV = \text{constant}$ at fixed $T$), [charles_law](#charles-law) ($V \propto T$ at fixed $p$), and the **combined** or **ideal gas law**:

$$
pV = nR T
$$

- $p$: Pressure
- $V$: Volume
- $n$: Number of moles
- $R$: [gas_constant](#gas-constant)
- $T$: Absolute temperature

Applications:
- Explaining how [boiling_point](#boiling-point) can shift under different pressures.
- Extending to mixtures via [daltons_law](#daltons-law).

<Alert type='warning'>
All these formulas assume gas behaves ideally, which can fail at very high pressure or very low temperature.
</Alert>
