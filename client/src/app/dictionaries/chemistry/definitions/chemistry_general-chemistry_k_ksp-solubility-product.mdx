<a id="ksp-solubility-product">Ksp (Solubility Product)</a> - The **equilibrium constant** for a **sparingly soluble ionic compound** dissolving in water. For a generic salt $ \text{A}_p \text{B}_q $:

$$
\text{A}_p\text{B}_q(\text{s}) \rightleftharpoons p\,\text{A}^{q-} + q\,\text{B}^{p+}
$$

Then,
$$
K_\mathrm{sp} = [\text{A}^{q-}]^p \,[\text{B}^{p+}]^q
$$

- Low $K_\mathrm{sp}$ means poor solubility.
- Ties to [hard_water](#hard-water) issues (like Ca<sup>2+</sup> deposits).
- Helps predict precipitation via the [common_ion_effect](#common-ion-effect) or changes in [solutions](#solutions).
- Reflects ionic-lattice stability, bridging [ionic_bond](#ionic-bond) strength and aqueous solvation.

<Alert type='note'>
Knowing $K_\mathrm{sp}$ is crucial for controlling scale formation, selective precipitation, and purification in chemical processes.
</Alert>
