<a id="dipole_moment">Dipole Moment</a> - A **vector quantity** ($\mu$) indicating the **separation of charge** in a molecule. It arises when **bonded atoms** have different [electronegativity](#electronegativity), creating partial charges.

$$
\mu = q \times d
$$

- $q$ = magnitude of partial charges
- $d$ = distance between charges

- Larger dipole moment typically means a more [polar_molecule](#polar_molecule).
- Intra- and [intermolecular_forces](#intermolecular_forces) often hinge on dipole interactions. 
- Single [covalent_bond](#covalent_bond) can have a dipole if it's polar.
