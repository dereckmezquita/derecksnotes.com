<a id="lewis-acid">Lewis Acid</a> - A **species** (atom, ion, or molecule) that can **accept** a pair of **electrons** to form a new bond. This definition broadens [arrhenius_acid](#arrhenius-acid) to non-proton donors.

- Often metal cations or electron-deficient molecules (e.g. BF<sub>3</sub>, AlCl<sub>3</sub>).
- Forms coordinate (dative) bonds with a [lewis_base](#lewis-base), sometimes generating a [complex_ion](#complex-ion).
- Overlaps with [dative_bond](#dative-bond) concepts: the acid is the acceptor.

<Alert type='important'>
Lewis acidity explains reactions beyond H<sup>+</sup> donors, encompassing transition-metal catalysis and non-protonic acid-base systems.
</Alert>
