<a id="beta-decay">Beta Decay</a> - A **radioactive** process where a **nucleus** emits a **beta particle** (electron $e^-$ or positron $e^+$). In $ \beta^- $ decay:

$$
\,^{A}_{Z}\text{X} \;\rightarrow\; \,^{A}_{Z+1}\text{Y} + e^- + \bar{\nu}_e
$$

- Charge conservation: Atomic number increases by 1, mass number ($A$) unchanged.
- Nuclear [binding_energy](#binding-energy) shifts can stabilise the nucleus.
- Must be a [balanced_equation](#balanced-equation) for nucleons and charge, distinct from typical chemical equations.
- Some isotopes ([isotope](#isotope)) rely on $\beta$ decay for transformation. The [bohr_model](#bohr-model) historically preceded full nuclear models but paved the way for atomic theory.
