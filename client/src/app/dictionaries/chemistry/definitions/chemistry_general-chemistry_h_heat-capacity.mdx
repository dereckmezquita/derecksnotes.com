<a id="heat_capacity">Heat Capacity</a> - The **quantity** of **heat** needed to **raise** an object's **temperature** by **1 degree** (1 K or 1Â°C). Symbolised $C$, or sometimes $C_p$, $C_v$ for constant pressure/volume contexts.

$$
C = \frac{q}{\Delta T}
$$

- **Molar Heat Capacity**: $C_m = C / n$, relates to substance-specific properties.
- **Connection**: In [calorimetry](#calorimetry), $C$ quantifies energy changes. Closely linked to changes in [enthalpy](#enthalpy) and how [entropy](#entropy) might vary with temperature.
- **Practical**: [ebulioscopy](#ebulioscopy) or any temperature-based technique must account for the system's or apparatus' heat capacity.
