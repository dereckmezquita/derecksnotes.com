<a id="kelvin_scale">Kelvin Scale</a> - The **absolute temperature scale**, using **absolute zero** ($0\,\mathrm{K}$) as the lowest limit. One kelvin increment equals one degree Celsius increment, but the scales offset by 273.15.

- **Relationship**: $T_{(\mathrm{K})} = T_{(^\circ\mathrm{C})} + 273.15$.  
- **Why 'Absolute'**: At $0\,\mathrm{K}$, theoretical molecular motion ceases (though quantum zero-point energy remains).  
- **Role**: Fundamental in [thermodynamics](#thermodynamics) and gas laws (e.g. [charles_law](#charles_law)).  
- **Statistical**: [entropy](#entropy) definitions rely on absolute temperature in JÂ·K<sup>-1</sup>.  
- The [avogadro_constant](#avogadro_constant) ties microstates to macroscale measurements, commonly at standard temperatures in Kelvin.

<Alert type='note'>
Scientific temperature calculations typically use kelvins, ensuring correct thermodynamic relations without negative absolute T values.
