# Ideas — quantitative trading review

> Running list. One sentence per idea, grouped. Working notes, not commitments.

## 1. Efficiency-frontier arbitrage ("anomaly transplantation")
- Core thesis: edges that decayed in efficient Western markets (crowded out by arbitrage capital) may still be alive in less-efficient frontier markets where that capital is absent.
- **US-history → frontier:** mine decayed US/Western anomalies, extract *why* each worked (the mechanism), then hunt that mechanism in crypto/Africa.
- **Frontier → US-history:** data-mine inefficiencies visible in crypto/frontier now, then match each to its historical Western analogue to learn the mechanism and predict its decay.
- **Friction is the moat:** prioritise barriers that are legal-but-hard (crypto's institutional/regulatory friction; capital controls, custody, illiquidity in frontier equities) — the wall that keeps competitors out is the alpha source.
- **Transfer the mechanism, not the label:** a strategy only ports if its structural cause (no arbitrageurs, slow info diffusion, retail dominance) is present in the target market.
- **Markets in scope:** crypto (primary — we own the data infra and it is the purest frontier) and accessible African / frontier equities.
- *Constraint — Russia parked:* the inefficiency is real but the barrier is a legal prohibition (sanctions), not a tradeable friction.

## 2. Cross-disciplinary method transfers (methods barely/never used in finance)
- **Branching-ratio criticality** (neuroscience): measure the market's distance to a self-exciting tipping point from your own fills to flag fragile, crash-prone regimes — *best single bet.*
- **Cyclostationary / FRESH filtering** (radar): exploit markets' hard calendar periodicities (opens, auctions, expiries, funding resets) instead of crude time-of-day dummies.
- **Adaptive shrinkage / local false-sign rate** (genomics): size each bet by the probability its *direction* is right and skip the coin-flips — cheapest, lowest-risk win.
- **Linear Inverse Model / POP** (climate/ENSO): a near-parameter-free multi-horizon forecaster plus a "most-likely-to-grow" precursor portfolio.
- **Entropy production / time-irreversibility** (thermodynamics): gauge distance from equilibrium (trend-friendly vs market-making-hostile) from broken detailed balance in price paths.
- **Box-Least-Squares period search** (exoplanets): detect rare, brief, repeating dips (liquidity holes, scheduled-event footprints) that Fourier methods smear.
- **Covariate-adaptive FDR** (genomics): let side-info (capacity, theory-backing) bend each strategy's significance bar — a near-free upgrade to strategy selection.
- **Whittle index / restless bandits** (operations research): allocate capital across many edges that decay whether or not you trade them.

## 3. Edges worth harvesting from the literature (potentially profitable)
- **Variance risk premium:** sell the systematically overpriced "fear" in option-implied vs realised variance — the most credible documented edge in the haul.
- **Net-of-cost multi-anomaly combination:** each known factor is weak after fees, but a diversified bundle yields a small, real return.
- **Trend / time-series momentum:** a modest, genuine edge that pays off most during crashes (a crisis hedge).
- **Cointegration / OU statistical arbitrage:** the dependable relative-value framework, but with documented post-2002 decay — a framework, not a guarantee.
- **Competing-risks trade-exit modelling** (medical survival analysis): treat stop / target / time / reversal as competing exits to raise per-trade expectancy.

## 4. Process / methodology as edge
- **Evaluation-first as a moat:** rigorous deflated-Sharpe / PBO / purged-CV discipline is itself an advantage when most participants fool themselves.
- **Execution alpha:** optimal execution and market-impact modelling (Almgren-Chriss, square-root law) is an underrated, defensible source of returns.
- **Market-trained foundation models over generic LLMs:** Kronos-style models built on price data help; generic chat-LLMs and zero-shot TSFMs largely do not.
